## 2. Onboarding & User Validation (Realistic, User-First, Secure)

### Critical Review and Refinement of Trust Levels

The three-level model is a solid foundation. Here's a refinement balancing friction, safety, and user experience:

*   **Level 0 – Unverified:**
    *   **Refinement:** Keep this. It's crucial for discoverability and passive engagement. Users can *browse all public Pings and Waves*, and potentially view public profiles, but no engagement features.
    *   **Risk:** Users might feel limited.
    *   **Mitigation:** Clear UI cues that encourage verification to unlock full features. "Verify your email to join the conversation!"
    *   **Security:** Low risk, as they can't post.

*   **Level 1 – Domain Verified (Core User):**
    *   **Refinement:** This remains the primary entry point for active participation.
    *   **Risk:** Fabrication of domain-matching emails. This is a real risk.
    *   **Mitigation:**
        *   **Magic Link (Strong):** The magic link sent to the *actual domain email* is the most robust initial verification for individual users. The user *must* have access to that inbox.
        *   **Initial Trust Assumption:** For a soft launch, assume *initial trust* in the magic link. If abuse is detected, you can escalate.
        *   **Temporary Restriction:** For *newly registered domains* (e.g., if someone registers `covenantunlversity.edu.ng`), flag them for manual review or restrict them to Level 0 until an admin can verify.
    *   **Security:** High importance. This verifies membership.

*   **Level 2 – Active Contributors (Trusted User / Talent Pool):**
    *   **Refinement:** This is an *internal reputation score*, not a security level. It's derived from system activity.
    *   **Risk:** Gamification abuse (e.g., self-upvoting circles).
    *   **Mitigation:** Algorithm should consider quality (e.g., high Surge on *resolved* Pings, positive sentiment on Waves, admin endorsements). Not just raw numbers.
    *   **Security:** Low impact on core system security, high impact on Talent Discovery feature integrity.

**Balance: Friction vs. Safety**
Your model correctly places friction at the point of *active participation* (Level 1). Browsing is frictionless. This is a good balance. The magic link makes Level 1 relatively low-friction for legitimate users.

**Steps that *must* be secure:** Verifying institution membership (Level 1). Without this, the system loses internal trust and integrity.

### Concrete, Step-by-Step User Onboarding Flow

**Goal:** Get users to Level 1 verification quickly and smoothly.

#### A. Typical CU Student Discovery

1.  **Discovery:** Student sees Echo link/QR code (poster, student council announcement, WhatsApp group). Clicks.
2.  **Landing Page:** "Welcome to Echo for Covenant University! Your voice, heard and acted upon." Prompts: "Join your campus conversation."
3.  **Sign Up (Email First):**
    *   Input field: "Your Covenant University Email" (e.g., `john.doe@stu.cu.edu.ng`).
    *   Small text: "We'll send a magic link to verify your institutional membership."
    *   Button: "Get Magic Link"
    *   *Alternative:* "Sign up with Google" (if their Google account is already linked to their CU email, this can be faster).
4.  **Email Sent Confirmation:** "Check your inbox! We've sent a magic link to `john.doe@stu.cu.edu.ng`. Click the link to complete verification and unlock full access."
    *   *Crucial for motivation:* Add a loading spinner or clear message: "While you wait, you can browse existing Pings and Waves here [link to Level 0 view] but won't be able to contribute until verified."
5.  **Magic Link Click:** Student clicks the link in their email.
6.  **Profile Creation (Optional First Use):**
    *   "Welcome, John!"
    *   "Set up your profile (optional):"
        *   Full Name (pre-filled if Google used, or suggested from email).
        *   Program/Department.
        *   Year of Study.
        *   Profile Picture.
    *   Check box: "I agree to the Terms of Service and Privacy Policy."
    *   Check box: "Yes, I'm open to being contacted by recruiters about my problem-solving contributions (optional)." (This is where Talent Discovery consent happens.)
    *   Button: "Complete Setup & Enter Echo"
7.  **Full Access:** Student now has Level 1 access, can create Pings, Waves, Surges.

#### B. Staff Member Onboarding

Same flow as A, but the system will recognize their staff-specific domain (e.g., `@covenantuniversity.edu.ng` vs. `@stu.cu.edu.ng`). Roles (admin/moderator) would be assigned post-verification by an existing institution admin, or by Echo team for initial setup.

#### C. Someone from Outside the Institution

1.  **Discovery:** Similar to above, but they attempt to join CU's space with a `gmail.com` or `yahoo.com` email.
2.  **Sign Up (Email First):** Input non-CU email.
3.  **Domain Check / Restricted Access:** System identifies non-CU domain.
    *   "It looks like you're trying to join the Covenant University Echo space with a non-institutional email. You can still browse public Pings and Waves, but won't be able to contribute."
    *   Offer: "If you have an institutional email, click here to try again."
4.  **Level 0 Access Only:** User proceeds to browse-only mode.

#### Avoiding Obvious Fake Accounts

*   **Magic Link:** Most effective barrier.
*   **Rate Limiting:** Limit sign-up attempts from a single IP address.
*   **Domain Blacklisting:** Maintain a small list of known spam/disposable email domains.
*   **Post-Verification Monitoring:** For very high-frequency posting from new accounts, flag for admin review.

#### Multitenancy

**Recommendation for a small team:** **Per-row `institution_id` with robust query filtering.**

*   **Pros:** Simpler to implement initially, easier to manage database migrations, shared infrastructure. Prisma handles this elegantly.
*   **Cons:** Requires diligent filtering (`WHERE institution_id = :current_institution_id`) on *every query* to prevent data leaks.
*   **Implementation:**
    *   Every core entity (`Pings`, `Waves`, `BulletinPosts`, `Memberships`, `Vendors`) should have an `institution_id` foreign key.
    *   Your API middleware should extract `institution_id` from the JWT and ensure all database operations are scoped to that ID.
    *   For admin actions, ensure the JWT's `institution_id` matches the target institution, and `is_admin` or `roles` allow the action.

Separate schemas or databases are overkill for your current stage and introduce significant operational complexity. Stick with row-level multitenancy until scaling demands dictate otherwise.

#### Evolving Later (Integration as Upsell)

*   **Roster Sync (CSV upload, SSO):**
    *   **Phase 1 (Manual CSV):** For Premium tier, admins can upload a CSV of `(email, name, role, department)` to pre-populate users, assign roles, and auto-verify. Your system matches by email.
    *   **Phase 2 (SSO):** Integrate with common university SSO providers (e.g., SAML, OAuth2). This is a paid Premium add-on. The `institution_id` and `roles` would come from the SSO assertion.
*   **Invite-Only Orgs:** Add a flag to `Institutions` table: `is_invite_only`. If true, new `Memberships` require an admin-generated invitation link or admin approval. This is an advanced feature for controlled environments.
*   **Admin Approval Paths:** For specific actions (e.g., sensitive Ping categories), add a `pending_review` status that requires an admin to approve before public visibility.

**Integration as an Upsell Language:**
"Echo is built to work immediately out-of-the-box, giving your institution transparent feedback without any IT overhead. For institutions seeking deeper integration, our Premium tier offers advanced features like Single Sign-On (SSO) and direct roster synchronization, streamlining user management and security within your existing IT ecosystem."

## 5. Anticipate Risks & Hard Questions

Here are some hard questions you'll face, with honest, consistent answers:

1.  **"Why not just use Google Forms or existing tools?"**
    *   **Answer:** "Existing tools like Google Forms are one-way and opaque – a 'brick wall' for users. Social media is noisy and unstructured. Echo is fundamentally different: it's *public within the organization*, structured, and transparently tracks issues to resolution. This fosters a unique level of trust and collective problem-solving that traditional tools simply can't offer."

2.  **"How will you keep people engaged?"**
    *   **Answer:** "Engagement is driven by seeing impact. Echo's transparency (tracking issues to resolution) and the social element (Surges, Waves) intrinsically motivate users. Beyond that, our 'Echo Campus Bulletin' provides a daily reason to check the app, and our 'Active Contributor' recognition (Level 2) offers a sense of achievement and potential for unique opportunities (Talent Discovery)."

3.  **"What about abuse, harassment, and privacy?"**
    *   **Answer:** "We have robust moderation tools for admins/moderators to address abuse. Our user verification process (magic links to institutional emails) reduces anonymity-driven malicious behavior. For privacy, we're explicit: all personal data is protected, and any advanced features like 'Talent Discovery' are strictly opt-in, with aggregated and anonymized data being the default for wider analytics."

4.  **"What if the Dean never acts on issues? Does Echo die?"**
    *   **Answer:** "No, Echo's value doesn't solely depend on immediate institutional action. While resolution is key, seeing others share the same pain (validation), discussing solutions (Waves), and knowing leadership *sees* the issue builds trust. Furthermore, our multi-sided business model allows us to generate revenue from vendors and recruiters even if institutional subscriptions are slow, giving us time to demonstrate the 'cost of inaction' to leadership with our data."

5.  **"How do you verify users and avoid fake accounts?"**
    *   **Answer:** "We use a magic link sent to institutional email addresses. Users must have access to that email to verify. This creates a high bar against fake accounts for active participation (Level 1). For initial browsing (Level 0), anyone can sign up, but they can't contribute."

6.  **"Will students trust you with their data?"**
    *   **Answer:** "Transparency is core to Echo. We will be clear about what data is collected, how it's used, and crucially, how it's *not* used. For features like 'Talent Discovery,' students must explicitly opt-in. We emphasize that Echo is for *their* benefit – to make their voices heard and improve their environment – not to exploit their information."

7.  **"What if institutions don’t want public internal criticism?"**
    *   **Answer:** "This is a common concern. We position Echo not as 'public criticism,' but as 'transparent, constructive feedback.' The criticism is already happening, often chaotically on social media. Echo channels that energy into a structured, resolvable format that builds trust and gives leaders an early warning system. We emphasize the long-term benefits of transparency for institutional reputation and stability over short-term discomfort."

8.  **"Is this just another survey tool?"**
    *   **Answer:** "No. Surveys are static snapshots; Echo is a living, dynamic conversation. It's social, allowing peer validation (Surges) and community-driven solutions (Waves). It tracks issues *to resolution* and fosters ongoing dialogue, unlike a one-off survey. It's less about data collection and more about creating an active ecosystem of change within the institution."

9.  **"How do you ensure data privacy across multiple institutions if it's all in one system?"**
    *   **Answer:** "Our backend is designed with robust row-level multitenancy. Each institution's data is strictly isolated by `institution_id` at the database level. Access controls ensure users and admins only see data relevant to their specific institution. Aggregated research data is always anonymized and never traceable to individual users or institutions without explicit consent."

10. **"How will you scale beyond universities to companies, which have different structures?"**
    *   **Answer:** "The core feedback loop (issues, solutions, transparency) is universal. For companies, 'Pings' become 'Employee Concerns' or 'Innovation Ideas,' 'Waves' are 'Team Solutions.' The 'Org Spaces' model is flexible for different company departments or locations. The multi-sided model (e.g., employee benefits vendors, talent acquisition for internal mobility) will also adapt. Our current university focus allows us to refine the core product before tackling the nuances of the corporate environment."
